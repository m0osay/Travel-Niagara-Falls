Niagara Falls

Two Countires, One Spectacular View

Buckle up, we will be exploring the amazing waterfall located in both the US and Canada. Niagara Falls is a collective name for three waterfalls that straddle the international border between the Canadian province of Ontario and the American state of New York. It is a breathtaking natural spectacle and a bucket-list destination for travelers worldwide.

About Ontario, Canada

Overview:

  • Area: 1.076 million km²
  • City Population (Niagara Falls, Ontario): Approximately 88,000
  • Country Population (Canada 2025): Estimated 40.1 million
  • Fun fact: Ontario is home to over 250,000 lakes, containing about one-fifth of the world's freshwater.

About New York, U.S.A

Overview:

  • Area: 141,300 km²
  • City Population (Niagara Falls, New York): Approximately 48,000
  • Country Population (U.S.A. 2025): Estimated 347.3 million
  • Fun fact: New York was the first state to require license plates on automobiles, but it did not issue them.

Travel Tips

Before you Travel

  • For the best weather, visit during the summer, or consider the spring and fall for fewer crowds.
  • Bring a waterproof jacket for the mist and comfortable walking shoes for exploring the parks on both sides.
  • Carry both Canadian and US dollars as each side uses its own currency.
  • Ensure you have a passport or enhanced driver's license to cross the border and see both sides of the falls.
